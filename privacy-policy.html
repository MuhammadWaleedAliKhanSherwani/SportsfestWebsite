Privacy Policy

Bolt Sportsfest Privacy Policy (2025)

This policy outlines how the Bolt Organizing Committee ("we," "us," or "our") manages your personal information during the Bolt Sportsfest 2025 event ("Sportsfest"). By registering for, volunteering at, or attending the Sportsfest, you agree to this policy.

1. What Information We Collect

We only collect information necessary to run the competition and ensure participant safety:

  • Registration Data: Your name, email, phone number, age/date of birth, gender, team name, and specific events you've signed up for.
  • Emergency & Safety Data: Emergency contact name and phone number, and relevant medical/allergy information provided voluntarily by you.
  • Media & Image Data: Photographs, videos, and audio recordings captured by our official media team or partners during the event.

2. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used strictly for the following purposes:

  • Event Operation: Processing registrations, creating heat sheets and schedules, calculating results, and communicating important event updates (e.g., weather delays, schedule changes).
  • Safety & Welfare: Providing necessary contact and medical information to on-site medical and safety personnel in case of an emergency.
  • Publicity & Promotion (Media Consent): Using official photos and videos to promote the Bolt Sportsfest and future events on our website, social media channels, and in post-event summaries.

3. Media and Publicity Consent

By participating in the Bolt Sportsfest, you grant the Organizing Committee permission to use your likeness (photos, video, and audio) for promotional and archival purposes related to the Sportsfest, without further compensation.

Opt-Out Provision: If you DO NOT wish to be photographed or videoed, please notify the registration desk upon check-in to receive a designated marker. While we will make every reasonable effort to exclude individuals with this designation from feature shots, we cannot guarantee exclusion from spontaneous crowd shots or general footage of the competition area.

4. Data Security and Sharing

Security: We maintain appropriate administrative and technical safeguards to protect your personal data from unauthorized access or misuse.

Limited Sharing: We do not sell your personal information. We only share necessary data with essential third parties directly involved in the event, such as:

  • Timing Companies: To ensure accurate results.
  • Medical Staff: For emergency care only.

5. Your Rights and Contact

You have the right to request access to the information we hold about you and request corrections to any inaccuracies.

Security Measures

Bolt Sportsfest 2025 Security Measures

Entry Control & Access Management

  • Check tickets/passes, perform consent-based bag checks, and prevent unauthorized access at all entry/exit points.
  • Position personnel in high-traffic areas and choke points to manage crowd flow and prevent overcrowding.
  • Use conflict resolution training to manage disruptive, intoxicated, or aggressive attendees, leading to non-violent removal if necessary.
  • Ensure all attendees and participants follow the Sportsfest rules and Terms & Conditions.
  • Secure the perimeters of restricted areas (VIP tents, staging areas, technical zones).
  • Act as the first point of contact for minor incidents and immediately escalate serious issues.

Armed Security Personnel

  • Station armed guards at main entrances, control centers, and critical infrastructure points to deter serious threats (Note: Armed personnel must comply with all local laws and licensing).
  • Operate armed personnel under a clear, legally compliant Use-of-Force Protocol, reserving lethal force as a last resort.
  • Ensure all armed personnel have current state/local licenses, firearms certifications, and proper insurance.

Explosive Detection & K9 Units

  • Conduct pre-event explosives detection (EOD) sweeps of the entire venue and check delivery vehicles using K9 units.
  • Deploy K9 units at entry perimeters for highly effective search and psychological deterrence against illegal substances or weapons.
  • Utilize highly visible K9 teams for patrols in perimeter and common areas to maximize their deterrent effect.

Command & Communication

  • Establish a central hub, a Unified Command Post, where leads of all security teams (unarmed, armed, police/K9) can coordinate operations.
  • Define explicit, rehearsed protocols for incident handoff from unarmed staff to armed security or law enforcement.
  • Utilize dedicated, reliable two-way radio channels and backup communication methods to link all security teams.
  • Have rehearsed Emergency Action Plans (EAPs) for scenarios like medical emergencies, fire, and large-scale evacuations.
